lesson.log
⟐ ORCHESTRATION LESSON Every agent generates confident, plausible-sounding numbers — and all are wrong at some rate. In a 3-agent pipeline with 10% hallucination rate per agent: P(error in final output) = 1 − (0.9)³ = 27% The orchestrator's primary job is verification, not generation. Future pipelines get a Verifier stage — a fourth agent or human reviewer — whose only job is to fact-check every benchmark, assumption, and off-brief drift before output reaches decision-makers.

CUSTOMER TRUST
Trust hinges on transparency + control. Players accept adaptation when it feels helpful (not manipulative). Price personalisation must be explicitly labelled as activity-based. An opt-out toggle in settings gives players agency. The risk is not "creepy" but "unfair" — a hard-difficulty or higher-price perception. Labelling and choice are the mitigations.
